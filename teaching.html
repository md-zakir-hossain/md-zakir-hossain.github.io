Teaching

Teaching Overview

I am a computing academic with extensive teaching experience across artificial intelligence, cyber security, data analytics, human-centred computing, and IT governance. My teaching spans undergraduate and postgraduate programs in Australia and internationally, delivered through face-to-face, blended, and fully online modes. I emphasise industry-aligned skills, ethical and responsible use of AI, and authentic assessment grounded in real-world professional practice.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in student-centred, applied learning that aligns clearly articulated learning outcomes with meaningful activities and assessments. I design learning experiences that balance strong theoretical foundations with hands-on problem solving, enabling students to develop both conceptual understanding and job-ready skills. I place particular emphasis on ethical awareness, security, and responsible use of technology, especially in AI-driven and data-intensive systems. My teaching is inclusive, reflective, and continuously refined based on feedback and evaluation.

Units Taught and Coordinated

Undergraduate

  • ICT204 Cyber Security – Lecturer
    Topics include threat management, endpoint security, cryptography and PKI, network and cloud security, and secure systems design. Practical laboratories incorporate tools and techniques such as NMAP, SQL injection, DoS/XSS attacks, and network traffic analysis using Wireshark. The unit aligns with CompTIA Security+ knowledge areas.
  • COMP2410 / COMP6340 Networked Information Systems – Lecturer \& Coordinator
    Large-cohort teaching with responsibilities including curriculum delivery, assessment design, and marking moderation. Designed a major assignment titled “Putting medical data on the Internet and securing it”, integrating networking architecture, threat modelling, privacy, and risk analysis.

Postgraduate

  • ICT309 IT Governance, Risk & Compliance – Lecturer
    Covers governance frameworks and standards including COBIT 2019, ITIL v4, CMMI, TOGAF 10, and ISO standards. Assessment tasks focus on applying governance, risk, and compliance principles to realistic organisational case studies.

International / Intensive Teaching

  • COMP2400 Database Systems – Lecturer (Shandong University, Weihai)
    Delivered as an intensive online unit using Zoom. Topics included database design, SQL, and data management, with emphasis on practical querying, data visualisation, and online student engagement.
