Zakir's Website

I am a Senior Research Fellow at the School of Electrical Engineering, Computing and Mathematical Sciences (EECMS), Curtin University, with a past affiliation with the Optus-Curtin Centre of Excellence in AI. I also serve as a Visiting Fellow at the Australian National University (ANU)’s Biological Data Science Institute (BDSI) and a Visiting Scientist at Data61, CSIRO. My research focuses on developing AI-driven solutions for health informatics, affective computing, and privacy-preserving AI applications. With over a decade of experience in interdisciplinary research and teaching, I have contributed to machine learning, computer vision, and human-centered AI through academic, industry, and government collaborations.

Prior to joining Curtin University, I worked as a Research Fellow at BDSI, ANU and as a Visiting Scientist with the Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence Future Science Platforms (MLAI FSP) and Agriculture and Food business unit at CSIRO. I also served as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Centre for Health Informatics (CHI), Macquarie University, Sydney. From 2018 to 2020, I worked as a Postdoctoral / Research Fellow and Lecturer at OHIOH (Our Health in Our Hands) Grand Challenge and the Research School of Computer Science (RSCS), ANU. Additionally, I worked as a Human-Centered Computing Support Officer at the RSCS, ANU at the end of my PhD. Before moving to Australia in 2015, I worked as a Lecturer and later as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical & Electronic Engineering (EEE) at Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (KUET), Khulna, Bangladesh. Prior to that, I began my academic career as a Lecturer in EEE at the University of Information Technology & Sciences (UITS), Dhaka, Bangladesh.

Since 2012, I have taught over 20 university courses and supervised numerous students, including PhDs, master’s, and honors students, at various institutions including the University of Information Technology & Sciences (UITS) and Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (KUET) in Bangladesh, as well as the University of Canberra, ANU, and Curtin University. I am an Associate Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (AFHEA), awarded by the UK Advanced Higher Education Academy. Through my teaching roles, I aim to foster critical thinking and innovation among students, drawing from my international experience and diverse academic background to enrich the learning environment.

My research focuses on developing advanced technologies to address health-related challenges through responsible AI and responsive AI frameworks. At Curtin University, I lead projects that create privacy-preserving AI approaches for applications such as affective computing, emotion recognition, and predictive models for managing human diseases. Previously, at ANU’s Biological Data Science Institute (BDSI) and as a Visiting Scientist at CSIRO, I developed biologically informed machine learning models for predicting diseases in salmon and agricultural traits in wheat. My research spans a range of health-related AI applications, from diagnosing COVID-19 via acoustic signals to building predictive models for individuals with chronic conditions like multiple sclerosis and diabetes.
